01 — Overview

Project Overview

RentFlow is a multi-role prop-fintech Real Estate platform created for Dubai's rental market. The core innovation: tenants pay monthly while investors pre-fund the annual lease and share the spread with the platform.

As the sole Senior UX/UI Designer, I mapped each user journey — Tenant, Agent, Landlord, Investor — crafted a unified responsive UI kit, and delivered end-to-end screens including admin panel and transactional email flows, turning a novel funding model into a seamless, production-ready experience.

02 — Problem

Problem Statement & Challenge

The Middle East real estate market is very challenging for newcomers and citizens without property. Almost 34% of Dubai tenants need financing for their annual rent — they can't afford one lump-sum payment upfront, which is the market standard.

The client came up with a new approach: let tenants pay monthly while investors pre-fund the annual lease and earn a yield. My challenge was to design a platform that made this novel financial concept feel simple, trustworthy, and intuitive for all four user types simultaneously.

Key insight

34% of Dubai tenants require rent financing — an untapped market with real demand for a monthly-payment model.

01
4 distinct user roles
Tenant, Agent/Landlord, Investor, and Admin — each needed a tailored flow without feeling like 4 separate products.
02
Novel financial model
Explaining yield-share and pre-funding clearly enough that users could trust and act on it — without legal jargon.
03
RTL & localisation
The platform needed to feel at home in Dubai's bilingual (Arabic/English) property market with RTL-ready design from day one.
03 — Discovery & Strategy

Discovery & Strategy

To ground the project in real-world context, I began with a SWOT analysis of leading Dubai real-estate platforms, mapping their strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.

Next, I ran a stakeholder workshop with the client to unpack the SWOT insights. We prioritized open questions and agreed to conduct at least five qualitative interviews with each core persona — tenants, agents, and landlords — while the client handled interviews with investors.

04 — Ideation → Wireframes → Validation

Design Process

After distilling insights from stakeholder and user interviews, I followed a structured four-step process to move from problem to polished product.

01
Define the feature set
Prioritized must-have capabilities based on recurring pain points and business value from research.
02
Set the visual direction
Built mood boards to align on tone, palette, and imagery. Trust and clarity were the north star.
03
Prototype fast
Sketched low-fidelity flows to validate IA and task completion before investing in hi-fi.
04
Polish the system
Selected typography and UI tokens, then moved to high-fidelity mockups for usability testing.
05 — Design Phase

Design System & UI Kit

Crafted an RTL-ready design system (8-pt grid, styles, typography). Delivered all assets, notes, and annotations to the client's development team. Designed high-fidelity screens with micro-interactions for booking, payment breakdown, and trust badges.
